PRIVACY POLICY - OUTSMART

Last Modified: December 2025

1. INTRODUCTION

Welcome to Outsmart (hereinafter “the Application”), an intelligent outreach application accessible via the Telegram bot @outReachExperto_bot and other channels.

The protection of your privacy is an absolute priority. This Privacy Policy describes:

  • What information we collect
  • How we use it
  • Who we share it with
  • How we protect it
  • Your rights

This Privacy Policy applies to all users of Outsmart, including our direct clients (Users/Clients) and our clients’ leads (Contacts/End-users).

2. DEFINITIONS

For better understanding, we define the following terms:

  • “We” / “Our” / “XLRA”: The organization that manages the Application
  • “Outsmart”: The Application
  • “Client/User”: The person who subscribes to and directly uses Outsmart (owner of the Telegram account)
  • “Contact/Lead/End-user”: The person represented in the Client’s data (the Client’s potential customers)
  • “Personal Data”: Any information that identifies, or can identify, an individual
  • “Processing”: Any operation on personal data (collection, storage, use, sharing, deletion)
  • “Data Controller”: The entity that determines how and why data is processed
  • “Data Processor”: The entity that processes data on behalf of a Data Controller
  • “Subprocessor”: A processor that works on behalf of another processor

3. INFORMATION COLLECTED

3.1 Data Collected from Clients (Direct Users)

When you sign up for Outsmart and use the Telegram bot, we collect and retain:

Identification Data:

  • Telegram account ID (unique user identifier)
  • First and last name
  • Email
  • Brevo API key
  • Google Access Token and Refresh Token

Preference and Configuration Data:

  • Communication preferences with your clients (tones, specific phrases, links, informational material)
  • Public relations management settings
  • Brevo API Key (provided by the user during sign-up)
  • Authorized integrations and connections (Google Sheets, Google Drive, Google Calendar)

Behavioral Data:

  • Timestamp of access and use of the Application
  • Choices in outreach flows (e.g., interest in discovery calls, follow-ups, etc.)
  • Engagement metrics related to your workflows

Note on Retention: We maintain access to user data as long as the account remains active. If the account becomes inactive for 7 consecutive days, we lose access to data (particularly associated Google Sheets). After 365 days of inactivity, we permanently delete the user’s information.

3.2 Data Collected from Contacts (End-user/Lead)

When a Client uses Outsmart to contact their leads, we collect the following data provided by the Client:

Lead Identification Data:

  • First and last name
  • Work phone number
  • Work email
  • Business address
  • Type of business (sector/industry)
  • Business name

Interaction and Engagement Data:

  • Email opens (timestamp and frequency)
  • Email replies (communication content)
  • Important relational data between Client and Contact (e.g., discovery call outcome, interest in site visit, etc.)
  • Timestamp of each interaction

Behavioral Information:

  • Engagement choices (interest in discovery calls, face-to-face meetings, etc.)
  • Business relationship status (prospect, in negotiation, client, etc.)

Important: Contact data remains archived in the Client’s Google Sheets. Outsmart does not directly retain Contact data on its servers.

3.3 Technical Tracking Data

We automatically collect:

  • Timestamp of all actions
  • Workflow execution data (status, results, errors)
  • API access logs

4. HOW WE COLLECT DATA

4.1 Direct Collection

  • Sign-up: When you create an account and authenticate via Google OAuth 2.0
  • Configuration: When you enter API keys and preferences in the sign-up form
  • Usage: When you use outreach flows and interact with the Application
  • Synchronization: When you insert data in the Google Sheet created by the app, and when you provide Gmail access via the initial Google sign-in

4.2 Automatic Collection

  • Email tracking pixel: We use invisible pixels to track email opens (on a thread basis, not individually)
  • Brevo Webhook: We receive automatic notifications of email replies
  • System Logs: We automatically record all actions in the system

4.3 Collection from Third Parties

  • Google: Account details via OAuth 2.0
  • Brevo: Email delivery data and interactions (via API provided by the Client)
  • Telegram: User profile information

5. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

5.1 For Clients (Direct Users)

We process your data based on:

  • Contract Performance: Necessary to provide the Outsmart service
  • Consent: You have consented to behavioral tracking and preferences
  • Our Legitimate Interest: Improving security, fraud prevention, business operations

5.2 For Contacts (End-user)

Contact data is processed by the Client (Data Controller) and Outsmart acts as Data Processor:

  • Contract Performance: The Client is responsible for obtaining Contact consent
  • Commercial Legitimate Interest: The Client’s perspective in contacting potential customers

Important Note: The Client is responsible for ensuring that they have the legal right to share Contact data with Outsmart and that they have obtained necessary consents according to applicable law (GDPR, CCPA, etc.).

6. USE OF DATA

6.1 Use of Client Data

We use Client data exclusively for:

  1. Service Delivery:

    • Account authentication and management
    • Execution of outreach flows
    • Storage and synchronization with Google Sheets, Gmail, Google Drive
    • Integration with Brevo for email sending and open tracking

  2. Security and Regulatory Compliance:

    • Fraud prevention and abuse
    • Compliance with legal obligations
    • Platform security protection

  3. What We Don’t Use:

    • Targeted marketing or advertising
    • Sale to third parties
    • Behavioral analysis for sale
    • Training machine learning models without explicit consent

6.2 Use of Contact Data

Contact data is used exclusively for:

  1. Outreach Operations:

    • Sending automated emails (via Brevo)
    • Engagement tracking (opens, clicks, replies)
    • Managing communication between Client and Contact
    • Documenting business relationship

  2. Platform Operation:

    • Synchronization with Client’s Google Sheets
    • Storage of communication exchanges
    • Recording of interactions

  3. What We Don’t Use:

    • Independent profiling
    • Sale of contacts
    • Use for purposes not specified by the Client

6.3 Use of Language Models (LLM)

When we use language models within outreach flows:

  • Model Selection: We select exclusively models that declare they do not collect, train on, or resell processed data
  • Privacy First: Data sent to language models is not used for training or analysis purposes
  • Transparency: We inform you when a flow uses language models

8. DATA STORAGE AND RESIDENCY

8.1 Where Data Is Stored

Contact Data

  • Stored in: Client’s Google Sheets
  • Residency: Google data centers (generally EU-USA depending on Client’s configuration)
  • Control: Under the direct control of the Client and their Google account

Workflow Execution Data

  • Stored in: n8n Cloud servers
  • Residency: Microsoft Azure, German West Central data center in Frankfurt (EU)
  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption at rest, FIPS-140-2 compliant
  • Access: Only to n8n systems, protected

Client Data (Preferences, API Keys, Logs)

  • Stored in: n8n Cloud Database
  • Residency: Microsoft Azure Frankfurt (EU)
  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption at rest
  • Backups: Encrypted and securely retained

8.2 Data in Transit

All data in transit is protected through:

  • TLS/SSL Encryption: All endpoints use HTTPS
  • Cryptographic Algorithms: Industry-standard encryption
  • Protected APIs: All API endpoints are protected and inaccessible to unauthorized actors

9. SECURITY MEASURES

9.1 Encryption

  • At Rest: AES-256 encryption for all stored data
  • In Transit: TLS 1.2+ for all transmissions
  • Algorithms: We use recognized cryptographic standards (FIPS-140-2 compliant)

9.2 Authentication and Access

  • OAuth 2.0: Secure authentication via Google
  • Session Management: Automatic timeouts for inactive sessions

9.3 Infrastructure Protection

  • Secure Hosting: n8n Cloud on Microsoft Azure with international compliance
  • Physical Security: Frankfurt data center with robust physical protection
  • Network Isolation: Services in private network, inaccessible to public internet
  • DDoS Protection: Protection against distributed attacks

9.4 Monitoring and Audit

  • Internal Audits: Conducted periodically upon request
  • Security Audits: Regular checks of security posture
  • Certifications: Compliant with SOC 2 Type 2 (n8n) and international standards
  • Incident Response: Established incident response procedures

9.5 Third-Party Compliance

  • Google: Compliance with GDPR, CCPA, SOC 2, ISO 27001
  • Brevo: Compliance with GDPR, ISO 27001, SOC 2
  • n8n: SOC 2 Type 2, Azure Compliance certifications
  • Microsoft Azure: GDPR, HIPAA, SOC 2, ISO 27001

9.6 What We Don’t Do

  • We never store passwords in plain text
  • We don’t share access credentials between systems
  • We don’t use hardcoded credentials in code
  • We don’t host data in non-certified data centers

10. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

10.1 Retention Period

Client Data

  • Active Retention: For the entire duration of the account and 7 days after inactivity
  • Automatic Deletion: After 365 days of account inactivity

Contact Data

  • Retention: Remains in Client’s Google Sheets (under their control)
  • Responsibility: Client is responsible for retention/deletion
  • We Don’t Store Directly: Outsmart does not independently retain Contact data

Workflow Execution Data (Residual)

  • Retention: 365 days on n8n servers
  • Automatic Deletion: After 365 days, definitive deletion of all residuals

Backups

  • Retention: Retained in line with main retention policy
  • Deletion: Permanently deleted after indicated periods

10.2 Account Deletion

If you choose to close your account:

  • All your personal data is deleted immediately
  • Workflow execution data is deleted after 365 days (normal retention cycle)
  • We retain no copies of your data after closure
  • You have the right to request deletion at any time

How to request it: Contact us via email at the support address (see Section 14 - Contacts)

10.3 Right to be Forgotten

According to GDPR and CCPA, you have the right to request complete deletion of your personal data. To exercise this right:

  1. Send a formal request to our support email address
  2. Specify which data you wish to be deleted
  3. We will respond to the request within 30 days (GDPR) or 45 days (CCPA)

11. YOUR RIGHTS

11.1 Rights Under GDPR (Europe)

If you are an EU resident, you have the following rights:

1. Right of Access: You can request a copy of all personal data we retain about you

2. Right of Rectification: You can correct inaccurate data

3. Right to Erasure: You can request deletion of your data (“Right to be Forgotten”)

4. Right to Data Portability: You can request your data in machine-readable format

5. Right to Object: You can object to specific types of processing

6. Restriction of Processing: You can ask to limit how your data is used

7. Right to Lodge a Complaint: You can file a complaint with your national Data Protection Authority

11.2 Rights Under CCPA (California)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights:

1. Right to Know: You can request which categories of personal data we collect

2. Right to Deletion: You can request deletion of personal data

3. Right to Opt-Out: You can object to the sale or sharing of data (note: we don’t sell data)

4. Right to Non-Discrimination: We cannot discriminate against you for exercising your rights

5. Right of Correspondence: You can designate an authorized agent to exercise your rights

11.3 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any right described above:

  1. Send a written request to email: mailto.xlra.emanuel+support@gmail.com
  2. Clearly specify which right you wish to exercise
  3. Provide sufficient information to identify yourself
  4. We will respond within legal timeframes (30 days GDPR, 45 days CCPA)
